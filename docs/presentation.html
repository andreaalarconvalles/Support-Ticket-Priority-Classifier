Hyperparameter Justification

Embedding dim: 64 Balances expressiveness vs. parameter count for 5.7K vocab
LSTM units: 128×2 Enough capacity to capture priority-relevant patterns
Dropout: 30% Prevents memorisation on ~11.4K training samples
Optimizer: Adam Adaptive LR; converges reliably on sequence tasks
Loss: Sparse CE Standard for integer-encoded multi-class classification
MAX_SEQ_LEN: 57 Fixed sequence cap; tickets over 57 tokens truncated at the end

09 — Regularisation

Overfitting Prevention Strategy

Dropout (30%)

Applied after the Bi-LSTM layer. During training, 30% of units are randomly zeroed per batch, forcing the network to learn redundant representations rather than memorising training examples.

Dropout rate chosen via Keras-Tuner search

Early Stopping

Training monitors validation loss after each epoch. If it fails to improve for a set patience window, training stops automatically — preventing the model from continuing to memorise training data after generalisation peaks.

Monitors val_loss · restores best weights

Stratified Split

Train/Val/Test sets are split with stratification on the priority label, ensuring each split has the same class distribution. This prevents accidentally validating on an easy subset and gives honest generalisation estimates.

70 / 15 / 15 % · stratified · seed 42

Additional Measures

  • No data leakage: deduplication runs before the split, preventing duplicate training samples from bleeding into test.
  • Classes are imbalanced (Low 21% / Medium 41% / High 39%), so balanced class weights are computed and passed to training — avoiding bias toward the majority classes.
  • Keras-Tuner automated hyperparameter search avoids manual over-tuning on the validation set.
